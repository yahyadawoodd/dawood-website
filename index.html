About Us — The Dawood Family

A Legacy Built
On Industry

The Dawood family started from modest origins when Ahmed Dawood began trading in pre-partition India, eventually joined by his brothers. At partition, the family opted to move to Pakistan — to develop, as Mr. Jinnah put it, the "grand opportunity" that is Pakistan. The family thus made the transition from business to industry, first establishing Dawood Cotton Mills Limited in 1952.

The industry expanded rapidly. Senior members of the family ensured that the education of all successive generations be given the utmost importance. The family today has more MBAs than any other family in Pakistan.

The family diversified through the setting up of Dawood Hercules Chemicals, followed by Transpak, Dawood Yamaha, Dawlance, and Descon Engineering — as well as philanthropic ventures including Dawood Public School for Girls and Dawood College of Engineering.

Continuing this proud tradition, Dawood Industrial Fasteners Private Limited was established to bring the same standards of excellence and reliability to Pakistan's growing industrial fastener sector — manufacturing high-strength bolts, nuts, axles, and precision components from our facility in Karachi's SITE industrial area.

"The Dawood name in business has always stood for integrity, hard work, and a commitment to Pakistan's development. We carry that legacy into every fastener we manufacture — batch after batch, order after order."

Quality Assurance

Precision Is
Our Standard

Every fastener leaving our facility passes through a structured, documented inspection regime — from raw material receipt to final dispatch.

01

Incoming Material Inspection

Raw steel rod verified for chemistry and mechanical properties against mill certificates before entering production.

02

Dimensional Control

Thread pitch, width across flats, height, and shank diameter checked at defined intervals using calibrated gauges.

03

Tensile & Hardness Testing

Mechanical properties tested every batch to confirm grade compliance (4.8, 8.8, 10.9 as applicable).

04

Coating Verification

Zinc plating thickness and hot-dip galvanizing coat weight measured per batch and recorded.

05

Thread Gauging

Thread form and fit confirmed using Go/No-Go gauges on every production run.

06

Pre-Dispatch Final Inspection

Visual, dimensional, and packaging verification before every outbound shipment.
